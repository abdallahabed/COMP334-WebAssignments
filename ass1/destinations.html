Explore Palestine's Destinations

Jerusalem

The spiritual heart of Palestine, known for its deep religious and historical importance to Islam, Christianity, and Judaism.

  • Al-Aqsa Mosque
  • Church of the Holy Sepulchre
  • Old City Markets
Did You Know?

Jerusalem has been continuously inhabited for over 5,000 years.

Bethlehem

The birthplace of Jesus Christ, attracting pilgrims from around the world.

  • Church of the Nativity
  • Manger Square
  • Milk Grotto
Did You Know?

The Church of the Nativity is one of the oldest continuously operating churches in the world.

Ramallah

A vibrant cultural hub filled with art galleries, music venues, and cafes.

  • Yasser Arafat Museum
  • Palestinian Cultural Palace
  • Al-Manara
Did You Know?

Ramallah hosts the largest annual cultural festival in Palestine every summer.

Hebron

Known for its glass and pottery crafts and the historic Ibrahimi Mosque.

  • Ibrahimi Mosque
  • Old City Market
  • Hebron Glass Factory
Did You Know?

Hebron is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, over 4,000 years old.

Nablus

Known for its traditional markets, soap-making industry, and delicious knafeh dessert.

  • Old City of Nablus
  • Mount Gerizim
  • Nablus Soap Factory
Did You Know?

Nablus has produced olive oil soap for over a millennium.
